### Revision 1

**What changed and why:**
The email was restructured to break out security details into bullet points and slightly formalize the tone ("Dear" vs. "Hello") to improve clarity and professionalism. It also expanded on compliance definitions to make the message more informative for a security-conscious audience.

**What improved, stayed the same, or got worse:**
Clarity and readability improved due to better formatting and specificity, while the core message and value proposition stayed the same. The tone became slightly more formal, which may feel less personal but more enterprise-appropriate.

### Revision 2

**What changed and why:**
The email shifted to a more consultative and customer-centric tone, emphasizing understanding of the client's priorities and reframing features as "foundations." It also slightly repositioned the CSM role as a "Support Manager" to align more with security and operational support.

**What improved, stayed the same, or got worse:**
The tone improved by feeling more tailored and strategic, which strengthens trust with enterprise buyers. However, changing "Customer Success Manager" to "Support Manager" could weaken the perceived strategic value of the role, while overall structure and clarity remained strong.
