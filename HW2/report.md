# Report

For this project, I focused on using AI to help write Customer Success emails. This is something Customer Success Managers (CSMs) do every day when responding to customers about product questions, onboarding, issues, or renewals. Writing these emails can take a lot of time, especially when many of them follow similar patterns. Because of that, this type of work is well-suited to AI. The goal is not to fully automate emails, but to create strong first drafts that save time and maintain consistent communication. However, since customer emails can be sensitive, especially in high-stakes situations, they still need to be reviewed by a human before being sent.

For the model, I used a Gemini (Google GenAI) model because it is easy to use, performs well for writing tasks, and integrates smoothly into a simple Python script. I also considered other models, such as OpenAI and Anthropic. In general, all of these models can generate good text, but they differ slightly in tone and how well they follow instructions. Gemini struck a good balance between speed, cost, and quality, making it a practical choice for this project.

I improved the system by refining the prompt step by step. In the first version, the instructions were very basic, simply asking the model to write a professional email. While the output was readable, it often sounded generic and lacked structure. In the second version, I added more guidance, including defining the role of a Customer Success Manager, setting the tone, and instructing the model to avoid making promises it could not guarantee. This made the emails sound more professional and consistent. In the final version, I added even more structure, including instructions for the model on how to organize the email and what to include. This made the responses clearer, more reliable, and better suited for real-world use. Overall, small changes to the prompt made a big difference in the quality of the output.

Even with these improvements, the system still has some limitations. In more serious situations, such as when a customer is upset or requesting a refund, the AI may respond with the right tone but still suggest something that should be handled by a human. There is also a risk that the AI could make assumptions if the input is unclear. While it usually handles tone well, it may miss some of the nuance needed in more emotional conversations. Another limitation is that the AI lacks knowledge of the specific customer or their history, which makes it harder to personalize responses. Because of these issues, the system should not be used on its own.

I recommend using this tool as a helper, not a replacement. It quickly drafts emails for Customer Success Managers to review and edit. It is best for simple or routine messages; complex or sensitive situations require personal attention. Companies adopting such tools should set clear usage guidelines.

This project shows that AI helps save time and improve communication consistency. However, AI still requires human judgment. The technology best supports people rather than replaces them.
